PARISH KNOWLEDGE BASE - SAINT JOSEPH THE WORKER PARISH
=========================================================

PARISH HISTORY
--------------
Saint Joseph the Worker Parish was established in 1965 by the Diocese of Bacolod. It was founded by the late Monsignor Ricardo Santos, who served the community for over 30 years. The church building was completed in 1970 and was inaugurated by Bishop Enrique Soriano. The parish serves approximately 5,000 registered families in the surrounding barangays.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQs)
-----------------------------------

Q: What are the office hours of the parish?
A: The parish office is open Monday to Friday, 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM, and Saturday from 8:00 AM to 12:00 NN. The office is closed on Sundays and public holidays.

Q: How do I register my family as a parish member?
A: Visit the parish office and fill out a Family Registration Form. Bring one valid ID and proof of residence (barangay certificate or utility bill). Registration is free.

Q: How far in advance should I schedule a wedding?
A: Couples should coordinate with the parish office at least 6 months before the intended wedding date. This allows time to complete the Pre-Cana seminar and all documentary requirements.

Q: Is there a fee for baptism?
A: Baptism is a sacrament and there is no fixed fee. However, a suggested donation of 500 pesos to the parish is appreciated.

Q: How many godparents are allowed for baptism?
A: According to church canon law, there should be a maximum of 2 godparents (1 male and 1 female). Only Catholic godparents who are confirmed and living in accordance with church law are accepted.

Q: Can non-parishioners get married in our church?
A: Yes, but they must get a letter of permission from their home parish. The bride or groom must also have a connection to this parish (e.g., they grew up here or a family member is a parishioner).

Q: Where can I get a Certificate of No Record of Marriage?
A: Go to the parish office during office hours and request it. Processing takes 3 to 5 business days. There is an administrative fee of 100 pesos.

Q: Does the parish offer Christian Burial for non-Catholics?
A: A funeral Mass may only be celebrated for baptized Catholics. Non-Catholics may have a Prayer Service at the discretion of the parish priest.

SACRAMENTAL PREPARATION PROGRAMS
----------------------------------

BAPTISM SEMINAR:
- Held every first Saturday of the month
- Time: 9:00 AM - 12:00 NN
- Parents and godparents must attend
- Venue: Parish Hall

PRE-CANA SEMINAR (Marriage Preparation):
- Held every second and fourth weekend of the month
- Friday: 6:00 PM - 9:00 PM
- Saturday: 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM
- Registration fee: 800 pesos per couple (includes workbooks and meals)

FIRST COMMUNION PREPARATION:
- Classes are held every Saturday from 2:00 PM to 4:00 PM
- Program runs for 6 months
- Children must be at least 7 years old

CONFIRMATION PREPARATION:
- Classes are held every Sunday from 1:00 PM to 3:00 PM
- Program runs for 1 full year
- Candidates must have received First Communion

PARISH MINISTRIES
-----------------
- Liturgical Ministry (Lectors, Commentators, Extraordinary Ministers of Holy Communion)
- Youth Ministry (Kabataang Kristiyano)
- Altar Servers Guild
- Basic Ecclesial Communities (BECs)
- Legion of Mary
- Charismatic Renewal Group (CFC)
- Parish Pastoral Council (PPC)
- Social Action Ministry (CARITAS)
- Choir Ministry (Adult and Youth Choir)

To join any ministry, inquire at the parish office or attend the group's regular meetings.

PARISH FACILITIES
-----------------
- Main Church: Can accommodate approximately 800 people
- Parish Hall: Available for rent for church-related events. Maximum capacity is 200 persons.
- Parish Garden: Available for outdoor gatherings with prior coordination
- Columbarium: The parish maintains a columbarium for the remains of departed parishioners. Inquire at the office for availability and fees.

SPECIAL DEVOTIONS AND EVENTS
------------------------------
- Daily Novena to Our Lady of Perpetual Help: Every Wednesday, 5:30 PM
- First Friday Mass and Exposition of the Blessed Sacrament: Every first Friday, 6:00 AM Mass followed by all-day Adoration
- Parish Fiesta (Feast of Saint Joseph the Worker): May 1 every year
- Simbang Gabi: December 16-24, 4:00 AM Mass
- Visita Iglesia: Coordinated by the parish every Holy Wednesday
